NHSN Reporting Reference

Reporting requirements, definitions, data formats, and submission protocols for the CDC National Healthcare Safety Network

Reporting Overview

The NHSN is the CDC's healthcare-associated infection tracking system. Hospitals enrolled in NHSN submit data on antimicrobial use (AU), antimicrobial resistance (AR), and healthcare-associated infections (HAI). AEGIS automates data collection, aggregation, and formatting for all three modules.

  1. Antimicrobial Use (AU) -- Monthly submission of Days of Therapy (DOT) and Defined Daily Dose (DDD) by agent, route, and location.
  2. Antimicrobial Resistance (AR) -- Quarterly submission of isolate counts with resistance phenotype classification using the first-isolate rule.
  3. HAI Events -- Individual event reporting for CLABSI, CAUTI, VAE, SSI, CDI, and MRSA bacteremia using NHSN event definitions.
  4. Denominator Data -- Monthly patient-days and device-days by location, used to calculate rates and device utilization ratios.
AU: Days of Therapy (DOT) and Defined Daily Dose (DDD)
MetricDefinitionNotes
DOT (Days of Therapy) Number of calendar days a patient receives a specific antimicrobial agent, regardless of dose. If a patient receives vancomycin on Monday and Tuesday, that is 2 DOT for vancomycin. Binary per day (0 or 1). Multiple doses in the same day = 1 DOT. DOT is the primary NHSN AU metric. Preferred over DDD for pediatric populations.
DDD (Defined Daily Dose) The assumed average maintenance dose per day for a drug used for its main indication in adults (defined by the WHO ATC/DDD system). Actual dose administered divided by DDD reference dose. DDD is designed for adult use. In pediatric settings like CCHMC, DOT is preferred because pediatric doses are weight-based and often far below adult DDD values.
DOT Rate (Total DOT / Patient-Days) x 1,000. Expressed as DOT per 1,000 patient-days. Allows comparison across locations of different sizes and census.
Antimicrobial Agent NHSN uses a specific list of antimicrobial agents grouped by class. Each agent has an NHSN code. Agents are reported by route (IV, PO, IM). Topical agents are excluded.
AR: First-Isolate Rule and Resistance Phenotypes
ConceptDefinition
First-Isolate Rule Only the first isolate of a given species from a patient in the reporting period (quarter) is counted. Duplicate isolates from the same patient are excluded to avoid over-counting. This is the standard NHSN/CLSI deduplication method for resistance reporting.
MRSA Methicillin-Resistant Staphylococcus aureus. Identified by oxacillin/cefoxitin resistance or mecA gene detection.
VRE Vancomycin-Resistant Enterococcus (E. faecium or E. faecalis). Identified by vancomycin MIC >= 32 mcg/mL.
ESBL Extended-Spectrum Beta-Lactamase producing Enterobacterales. Identified by confirmatory testing (ceftriaxone/ceftazidime + clavulanate).
CRE Carbapenem-Resistant Enterobacterales. Identified by resistance to meropenem, imipenem, or doripenem (MIC >= 4 mcg/mL) or positive carbapenemase test.
%R (Percent Resistant) (Resistant first-isolates / Total first-isolates) x 100 for a given organism. Reported per organism-drug combination per quarter per location.

CDA Format (HL7 Clinical Document Architecture)

NHSN accepts data in HL7 CDA R2 (Clinical Document Architecture, Release 2) format. CDA documents are XML files structured with a header (patient, encounter, facility identifiers) and a body (structured entries for each reportable data element).

ComponentDescription
CDA Header Patient demographics, encounter identifiers, facility OID, reporting period, document type.
CDA Body Structured sections containing coded entries (SNOMED, LOINC, RxNorm) for each reportable element.
Facility OID Unique Object Identifier assigned to the facility by NHSN during enrollment. Required in all submissions.
Location Mapping NHSN locations (CDC location codes) must be mapped to facility unit names. This mapping is configured in settings.
Validation CDA documents are validated against NHSN-specific schematron rules before submission. AEGIS validates locally before sending.
DIRECT Protocol

DIRECT (Direct Interoperability for Exchange of Clinical Transactions) is a secure, standards-based messaging protocol used to transmit CDA documents electronically to the NHSN. It uses S/MIME encryption over SMTP with X.509 certificates for authentication.

SettingDescription
DIRECT Address The facility's DIRECT email address (e.g., facility@direct.nhsn.cdc.gov). Issued during NHSN DIRECT enrollment.
NHSN Endpoint The NHSN DIRECT receiving address. CDA documents are sent as MIME attachments to this address.
Certificate X.509 certificate for S/MIME encryption. Must be issued by a DIRECT-compatible trust anchor.
Trust Anchor Root CA certificate that both sender and receiver trust. NHSN provides their trust anchor during enrollment.
MDN (Message Disposition Notification) Acknowledgment message returned by NHSN confirming receipt (processed/failed). Used for audit trail.
Data Sources
Data TypeSourceCadence
AU (DOT/DDD) Clarity (Epic reporting database) Nightly batch job. Monthly aggregation.
AR (Isolates) Clarity (microbiology tables) Nightly batch job. Quarterly aggregation with first-isolate deduplication.
HAI Events AEGIS HAI Detection module (FHIR-sourced) Real-time. Events flagged by HAI detection are available for NHSN reporting.
Denominators Clarity (ADT/device tracking tables) Nightly/weekly batch job. Monthly aggregation by location.
Reporting Schedule
ModuleFrequencyDeadline
AU Monthly Due within 3 months after the end of the reporting month.
AR Quarterly Due within 3 months after the end of the reporting quarter.
HAI Events Monthly Due within 30 days after the end of the reporting month (CMS requirement).
Denominators Monthly Submitted alongside HAI events. Due within 30 days of month end.
