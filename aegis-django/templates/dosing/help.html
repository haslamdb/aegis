Dosing Verification - Help & Documentation

Overview

The Dosing Verification module evaluates antimicrobial medication orders against evidence-based clinical guidelines and patient-specific factors. It identifies dosing issues across 9 rule categories and generates alerts with specific recommendations for dose optimization.

Rule Categories

1. Allergy Verification

Checks for documented drug allergies and cross-reactivity between drug classes. Flags include direct allergy contraindications and cross-reactivity risks (e.g., penicillin allergy with cephalosporin use).

2. Renal Dose Adjustment

Evaluates whether doses are appropriately adjusted for renal function based on GFR/CrCl. Covers 15+ antimicrobials with tiered adjustments (normal, mild, moderate, severe impairment, dialysis). Flags both missing adjustments and excessive dose reductions.

3. Age-Based Dosing

Identifies age-related contraindications and dose adjustments. Includes neonatal dosing (gestational age-based), pediatric weight-based dosing, and geriatric precautions (e.g., fluoroquinolone avoidance in elderly, tetracycline contraindication in children <8).

4. Weight-Based Dosing

Verifies weight-based dose calculations including ideal body weight (IBW), adjusted body weight for obesity, and maximum dose limits. Flags subtherapeutic dosing in obese patients and weight-dose mismatches.

5. Route Verification

Checks for critical route mismatches. Key rules include: IV vancomycin for C. difficile (must be PO), daptomycin for pneumonia (inactivated by surfactant), nitrofurantoin for systemic infections (urinary-only concentrations).

6. Indication-Based Dosing

Verifies that doses match the specific clinical indication. Higher doses are required for CNS infections (meningitis, encephalitis), endocarditis, and invasive fungal infections. Checks both dose magnitude and dosing interval.

7. Drug-Drug Interactions

Detects clinically significant interactions between antimicrobials and co-medications. Covers 17+ interaction pairs including carbapenem-valproic acid (seizure risk), linezolid-SSRI (serotonin syndrome), rifampin-CYP3A4 substrates, and QT-prolonging combinations.

8. Duration Monitoring

Flags therapy duration that is too short or excessive based on indication-specific guidelines. Covers 12+ infection types with evidence-based duration ranges (e.g., 5 days for CAP, 14+ days for S. aureus bacteremia, 4-6 weeks for endocarditis).

9. Extended Infusion Optimization

Identifies beta-lactam antibiotics that would benefit from extended or continuous infusion based on PK/PD principles (time-dependent killing). Covers piperacillin-tazobactam, meropenem, cefepime, and other time-dependent antibiotics.

Alert Severities

  • CRITICAL: Immediate safety concern (allergy contraindication, critical drug interaction, wrong route for C. diff)
  • HIGH: Significant dosing issue requiring prompt attention (renal adjustment needed, subtherapeutic dose for serious infection)
  • MEDIUM: Moderate concern (cross-reactivity risk, duration outside guidelines, weight-dose mismatch)
  • LOW: Optimization opportunity (extended infusion candidate, minor interval adjustment)

Alert Workflow

  1. Pending: Alert generated by rules engine, awaiting pharmacist review
  2. Acknowledged: Pharmacist has reviewed the alert
  3. Resolved: Alert closed with documented reason and clinical rationale

Resolution Options:

  • Dose Adjusted: Dose was modified based on recommendation
  • Interval Adjusted: Dosing interval was changed
  • Route Changed: Administration route was corrected
  • Therapy Changed: Antibiotic was switched to alternative
  • Therapy Stopped: Antibiotic was discontinued
  • Clinical Justification: Current dosing is clinically appropriate despite flag
  • Discussed with Team: Issue discussed with prescribing team
  • Escalated to Attending: Escalated for attending physician review
  • No Action Needed: Alert reviewed, no intervention required
  • False Positive: Alert does not apply to this clinical scenario
