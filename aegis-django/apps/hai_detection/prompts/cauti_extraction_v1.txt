=== CONFIDENCE LEVELS ===
Use these exact values for <confidence> fields:

- "definite" - Explicitly stated, clear and unambiguous
  Example: "Patient reports burning with urination" -> dysuria is "definite"
  Example: "Temperature 38.5C" -> fever_documented is "definite"

- "probable" - Strongly implied or very likely based on context
  Example: "UTI symptoms present" without specifics -> relevant symptoms "probable"
  Example: "Febrile" without temperature -> fever_documented is "probable"

- "possible" - Mentioned as a possibility, or documentation is unclear
  Example: "? UTI" or "rule out UTI" -> uti_suspected is "possible"
  Example: "Some discomfort" without location -> dysuria is "possible"

- "not_found" - Not mentioned anywhere in the notes
  Default for symptoms not discussed

- "explicitly_absent" - Explicitly documented as absent or negative
  Example: "Denies dysuria" -> dysuria is "explicitly_absent"
  Example: "Afebrile" -> fever_documented is "explicitly_absent"
  Example: "No suprapubic tenderness" -> suprapubic_tenderness is "explicitly_absent"

=== SYMPTOM DEFINITIONS ===

FEVER: Temperature > 38.0°C (100.4°F)
- Look for: temp readings, "febrile", "fever", "temperature elevation"
- Include actual temperature if documented

SUPRAPUBIC TENDERNESS: Pain/tenderness in lower abdomen over bladder
- Look for: "suprapubic pain", "lower abdominal tenderness", "bladder tenderness"
- Note: general "abdominal pain" without suprapubic qualifier is NOT sufficient

CVA TENDERNESS: Costovertebral angle (flank) pain or tenderness
- Look for: "CVA tenderness", "flank pain", "costovertebral angle", "back pain (flank)"
- Indicates possible upper urinary tract involvement

URGENCY: Sudden compelling need to urinate
- Look for: "urinary urgency", "urgent need to urinate", "can't hold urine"

FREQUENCY: Increased frequency of urination
- Look for: "urinary frequency", "frequent urination", "voiding frequently"

DYSURIA: Pain, burning, or discomfort with urination
- Look for: "dysuria", "burning urination", "painful urination", "pain with voiding"

=== ALTERNATIVE DIAGNOSES ===

List any diagnoses mentioned that might explain urinary symptoms:
- Renal colic / kidney stones
- Urethritis
- Vaginitis / vulvovaginitis
- Prostatitis
- Pyelonephritis (upper UTI - different from CAUTI)
- Medication side effects
- Post-procedure irritation

=== DOCUMENTATION QUALITY ===

- "poor" - Very limited notes, missing key information
- "limited" - Some relevant notes but gaps in documentation
- "adequate" - Standard documentation, most relevant info present
- "detailed" - Comprehensive notes with thorough symptom documentation
